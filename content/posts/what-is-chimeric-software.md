# What is Chimeric Software?

**Chimeric software** is a concept born from the retrocomputing community that embraces the capabilities of modern software engineering
while asking a simple but fascinating question:

**What if?**

What if a particular piece of software had existed decades earlier? 
What if it had been designed to run on the computers of the 8-bit and 16-bit era? 
What would it have looked like, how would it have behaved, and what compromises or innovations would its creators have made?

Rather than simply recreating vintage software, chimeric software imagines an alternate technological history. Often times assisted by the AI.
It blends ideas from different platforms, eras, and design philosophies into something that feels both familiar and entirely new.
One example is **GEOBENCH** [GEOBENCH](https://github.com/salvogendut/geobench): a chimeric fusion of **GEOS** and the **Amiga Workbench**,
reimagined as if it had been developed for the **Amstrad CPC** or **MSX** computers. It is not a clone of either system, but an exploration
of what might have been—a software artifact from a parallel timeline where the limitations of classic hardware inspired entirely new solutions.
Chimeric software celebrates imagination, technical ingenuity, and the enduring charm of retro computers by turning the question *"What if?"* into working software.
